Updated: 18-Sep-2002 Week of 16-22 September 2002

16 Sept. 2002

Gorbachev continues to deny the involvement of the Chernobyl incident in the development of Black Cholera

"I repeat once again, according to our research conducted by the great Soviet minds, the Chernobyl Incident could not have been the trigger for the Cholera mutation. I assure you, your accusations are baseless!"
- Mikhail Gorbachev

However, according to independent studies, the amount of radiation released during the Chernobyl Incident is enough to start the mutation process that led to the formation of a new organism - Black Cholera, which has now destroyed almost the entire population of Chernorussia and the South-eastern part of Czechoslovakia.

The loss of Chernorussia was an absolute blow to the World Community, the negligent attitude of the Warsaw Pact Countries to such a serious problem, hiding it from everyone, and led to the destruction of more than 98% of the country's population. The NATO mission in the Czechoslovak region is designed to prevent a repeat of the Chernorussia scenario. On this map, you can see the regions that are susceptible to Black Cholera infection. The blue region is a completely isolated area with an evacuated civilian population. The yellow zone is a high-alert zone, and finally the green zone, a safe space that is not affected by Black Cholera.

In addition, the world community will seek admission to the Chernobyl Incident Zone in order to obtain more accurate data on the radiation background in the region.

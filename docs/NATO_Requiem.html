Background information

Following the approved plan, several detachments, on the morning of November 30, 2002, will be sent to the south-eastern region of Czechoslovakia to clean up the territories from infected and disinfect reservoirs, this operation has been carried out steadily since 1988, due to the scale of the epidemic and the infected territory, it is not possible to completely eliminate Cholera in this region. Cleaning will be subjected to such cities as Titanovo, the Brat and Klokovec.

However, Operation Requiem is intended to lay the foundation for the subsequent efforts to eliminate Black Cholera and its consequences. During the operation, the latest means of treatment and disinfection will be used.

Operation Requiem was initiated by the President of Czechoslovakia, Vaclav Havel. The operation will begin on November 30, 2002 and will last, according to experts, about a year.

Additional Information:
  • The cholera epidemic allegedly began during the military conflict with the Warsaw Pact countries in 1985-1986.

  • Due to the fact that Black Cholera is transmitted through blood and contact with the fluids of infected people, the cleanup teams will be given appropriate equipment.
